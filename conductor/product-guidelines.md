# Product Guidelines: FitQuest

## Tone of Voice (Prose Style)
- **Epic and Encouraging:** The app acts as a supportive Game Master guiding a hero on their journey.
- **Narrative-Driven:** Interface copy should use RPG terminology (e.g., "missoes" instead of "Tasks", "Masmorras" instead of "Gyms", "XP" instead of "Points").
- **Positive Reinforcement:** Focus on celebrating effort and progress rather than penalizing missed days.

## Visual Branding and Aesthetic
- **Pen and Paper Aesthetic:** The UI should evoke a classic tabletop RPG feel, utilizing hand-drawn style characters, sketch-like elements, and parchment-like backgrounds.
- **Stitch Design Integration:** Incorporate design metadata and UI tokens from the Stitch platform to ensure the interface perfectly matches the intended "pen and paper" visual identity. 
- **Typography:** Use fonts that mimic neat handwriting or classic typewriter styles to complement the tabletop aesthetic.

## UX Principles (Feedback)
- **Immediate and Flashy:** Provide immediate, flashy micro-animations for every action and milestone to give continuous positive reinforcement.
- **Reactive State:** As the user takes PASSOS in the real world, the in-app XP bar and progress indicators should react in real-time.
- **Clear Call-to-Actions:** Despite the gamified UI, core fitness actions (like starting a workout) must remain highly visible and easy to interact with.

## RPG Complexity
- **Lightweight and Accessible:** Keep the RPG elements simple so they don't overwhelm casual users.
- **Simple Leveling:** A straightforward XP system where PASSOS and workouts translate directly to level progress.
- **Easy-to-Understand Items:** Use simple, universally understood items rather than complex stat modifiers or deep skill trees.
